## Understanding the Stalwart Ability Mechanics

The Stalwart ability is a signature trait primarily associated with the Duraludon evolutionary line. Its primary function is to ignore the effects of moves and abilities that redirect attacks. In a standard doubles match, a common strategy is to use a "support" Pokémon with high bulk to draw fire away from a "sweeper" using moves like Follow Me. A **stalwart ポケモン** ignores this redirection entirely.

When a Pokémon with Stalwart selects a target, the move will hit that specific target even if the opponent uses a redirection move. This also applies to abilities that draw in moves of a certain type, such as Storm Drain (Water) or Lightning Rod (Electric).

| Feature | Effect Under Stalwart |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Follow Me / Rage Powder** | **Ignored**; the user hits the original target. |
| **Ally Switch** | **Ignored**; the user hits the original slot/target. |
| **Storm Drain Ability** | **Ignored**; Water moves hit the intended target. |
| **Lightning Rod Ability** | **Ignored**; Electric moves hit the intended target. |
| **Spotlight Move** | **Ignored**; the effect is bypassed. |

> **💡 Tip:** Stalwart does not ignore protection moves like Protect, Detect, or Spiky Shield. It only bypasses the "redirection" aspect of the opponent's strategy.

## Strategic Applications in the 2026 Meta

The prevalence of redirection in 2026 is at an all-time high. With the return of powerful support units like Amoonguss and Ogerpon-Wellspring, the ability to bypass Rage Powder and Follow Me is invaluable. A **stalwart ポケモン** acts as a hard counter to "Protect the Castle" archetypes where a fragile but dangerous attacker is hidden behind a bulky redirector.

### Bypassing Storm Drain and Lightning Rod
In 2026, many Rain and Sun teams rely on Storm Drain (Gastrodon/Tatsugiri) or Lightning Rod (Raichu/Rhyperior) to protect their core members from super-effective hits. Archaludon, when equipped with Stalwart, can fire off a powerful Thunderbolt or Flash Cannon directly at the intended target, completely ignoring the "magnetic" pull of these abilities.

### Counters to Common Redirection Users
1. **Amoonguss:** Often uses Rage Powder to let its partner set up. Stalwart ignores this, allowing you to hit the partner with a Dragon Pulse or Flash Cannon.
2. **Ogerpon (Wellspring Mask):** Uses Follow Me to disrupt your flow. Archaludon can ignore the mask's effect and target the threat next to it.
3. **Clefable/Indeedee-F:** These are staple Follow Me users in Psychic Terrain teams. A **stalwart ポケモン** renders their primary defensive utility useless.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful of "Mirror Armor" or "Intimidate" cycles. While you can hit your target, your stats can still be lowered by other abilities or moves that don't rely on redirection.

## How to Counter a Stalwart User

While the **stalwart ポケモン** is powerful, it is not invincible. If you are facing an Archaludon or Duraludon, you must shift your strategy from redirection to direct mitigation.

1. **Focus Fire:** Since they ignore redirection, you can't hide your Pokémon. Instead, use both of your active Pokémon to target the Stalwart user and knock it out before it moves.
2. **Type Disadvantage:** Both Duraludon and Archaludon are weak to Fighting and Ground moves. High-speed Ground types like Great Tusk or Landorus-Therian can often OHKO them before they can utilize their ability.
3. **Special Bulk:** Most Stalwart users are Special Attackers. Bringing in a Special Wall like Blissey or a Pokémon with high Special Defense can nullify their offensive pressure.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Stalwart ignore the effects of Ally Switch?**

A: Yes. In the 2026 competitive ruleset, if an opponent uses Ally Switch to swap positions, a **stalwart ポケモン** will still track and hit the original target it was locked onto, effectively ignoring the swap.

**Q: Is Stalwart better than Stamina on Archaludon?**

A: It depends on the format. In Singles, Stamina is often better for physical bulk. However, in Doubles (VGC), Stalwart is generally considered superior because bypassing redirection is a unique and powerful tactical advantage that can win games on turn one.

**Q: Does Stalwart allow moves to hit through Protect?**

A: No. Stalwart only ignores redirection (moves/abilities that change the target). It does not bypass protection moves like Protect, Wide Guard, or Iron Guard. You would need a move like Feint or an ability like Unseen Fist for that.

**Q: Can I use an Ability Patch to get Stalwart?**

A: Stalwart is typically one of the standard abilities for Duraludon and Archaludon. If your Pokémon has its Hidden Ability and you want Stalwart, you may need to breed a new one or use an Ability Capsule if Stalwart is in the secondary standard slot. Always check the specific game version's ability slots for the most accurate info in 2026.
