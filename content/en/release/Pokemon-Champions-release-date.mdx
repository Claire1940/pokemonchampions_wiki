## Core Gameplay: Recruitment and Victory Points

One of the most radical departures in Pokemon Champions is how you acquire your team. There is no tall grass to run through; instead, players use the **Recruit** system. Every day, you can choose one Pokemon from a random selection to join your roster. However, these recruited Pokemon are initially temporary, remaining available for only one week of play.

To make a Pokemon a permanent member of your team, or to customize their loadout, you must earn **Victory Points (VP)**. VP is the primary currency earned through ranked, casual, and private battles.

### How to Spend Victory Points (VP)
*   **Permanent Recruitment:** Add a temporary Pokemon to your shop for permanent purchase.
*   **Move Tutoring:** Swap out moves to perfect your coverage.
*   **Nature Modification:** Adjust a Pokemon's nature to optimize their stat spreads.
*   **Held Items:** Purchase competitive staples like Leftovers, Choice Specs, or Life Orbs.
*   **Mega Stones:** Unlock the power of Mega Evolution for compatible species.

## The New Stat Formula: No More EVs

In an effort to make the game more accessible and focus on tactical decision-making rather than tedious grinding, the developers have completely overhauled the stat system. Effort Values (EVs) have been removed entirely. In their place, players use Stat Points (SP) to manually adjust their Pokemon's strengths. 

Furthermore, Individual Values (IVs) are now fixed at 31 for every Pokemon in the game. This ensures a level playing field where matches are decided by strategy rather than "perfect" breeding.

| Stat Component | Pokemon Champions System | Impact on Meta |
|---|---|---|
| **IVs** | Fixed at **31** | No more "0 Speed" Trick Room builds. |
| **EVs** | **Removed** | Training is instant and reversible. |
| **Stat Points (SP)** | Manually Allocated | Allows for precise bulk or speed tiers. |
| **Natures** | **10% Multiplier** | Remains a core part of optimization. |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Because IVs are fixed at 31, moves like Foul Play will always deal maximum damage based on the target's attack stat, and Trick Room users can no longer "underspeed" opponents by having a 0 Speed IV.

## Pokemon Home Integration

For long-time fans, the most important feature following the **Pokemon Champions release date** is the integration with Pokemon Home. You can "lease" Pokemon from your Home collection into Champions. 

When you transfer a Pokemon from Home:
*   Their original EVs are wiped (but restored when sent back to Home).
*   Their IVs are adjusted to the game's standard 31.
*   They retain their Nature, which can be modified using VP within the game.

This allows players to use their favorite Shiny Pokemon or event-exclusive mons in a competitive setting without ruining their stats for other games in the series. You can learn more about the latest connectivity updates on the [official Pokemon news portal](https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-news/).

## Battle Modes and Ranking System

Pokemon Champions offers three primary ways to play, catering to both the hardcore competitive crowd and casual fans.

*   **Ranked Battles:** The meat of the game. Face off against players of similar skill levels to climb from the Beginner Tier to the Master Ball Tier. Each season features preset rules and restricted Pokemon lists.
*   **Casual Battles:** A low-stakes environment where wins and losses aren't recorded. This is the perfect place to test out "spicy" off-meta teams or practice with new Mega Evolutions.
*   **Private Battles:** Create a room with a specific ID to battle friends or host your own community tournaments.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the official Pokemon Champions release date for mobile?**

A: While the Nintendo Switch version launched on April 8, 2026, the mobile version for iOS and Android is scheduled for a June 2026 release. Cross-play and cross-progression are expected to be supported.

**Q: Do I need to pay for a subscription to play?**

A: Pokemon Champions is a free-to-download title. While there are in-game purchases for cosmetics and "Quick Coupons" to speed up recruitment, all competitive elements can be unlocked through gameplay and earning Victory Points (VP).

**Q: Can I use my existing competitive teams from Pokemon Scarlet and Violet?**

A: Yes, via Pokemon Home. However, remember that their EVs will be reset to 0 and their IVs will be set to 31 once they enter the Pokemon Champions environment. You will need to re-allocate their Stat Points (SP) within the game.

**Q: Will other gimmicks like Terastalization be added?**

A: Yes. While the game launched with Mega Evolution, the "Omni Ring" lore suggests that Terastalization, Z-Moves, and Gigantamax will be introduced in future seasonal updates to keep the meta fresh.
