## Mega Excadrill and the Piercing Drill Ability

In the current version of Pokemon Champions, Mega Excadrill has been granted the ability **Piercing Drill**. Veteran players will recognize this as a functional mirror to the official Unseen Fist ability, though it has been specifically tailored for the Excadrill line. This ability ensures that if Mega Excadrill uses a contact move, the target cannot hide behind protection.

| Ability | Effect | Contact Required? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Piercing Drill** | Hits through Protect, Detect, and Spiky Shield | **Yes** |
| **Unseen Fist** | Standard version; hits through protection | **Yes** |
| **Sand Rush** | Doubles Speed in Sand (Base Excadrill) | No |

The strategic value of this cannot be overstated. In VGC-style doubles, "Protect" is often the most used move to stall out weather turns or Trick Room. Mega Excadrill ignores these stalls, forcing the opponent to either switch or take massive damage.

> **💡 Tip:** Remember that Piercing Drill only works with contact moves. While Earthquake is a staple for Excadrill, it is a non-contact move and will still be blocked by Protect. Use moves like Iron Head or Drill Run to utilize the ability effectively.

## Strategic Matchups in 2026

The current meta revolves around weather wars. While Sand is powerful, it faces stiff competition from Rain (Pelipper/Palafin) and Sun (Charizard Y/Whimsicott).

### Handling the Rain Matchup
Rain is arguably the hardest counter to a Sand team. Palafin, even after its recent nerfs, remains a massive threat with Jet Punch and Flip Turn. 
*   **The Play:** Lead with Reuniclus and Incineroar. Use Fake Out to stop the initial Flip Turn and set up **Trick Room**. Under Trick Room, Tyranitar can outspeed Palafin and threaten a KO with Thunder Punch.

### Countering Sun Teams
Charizard Y is a frequent sight on the 2026 ladder. Its ability to set Sun and fire off Heat Waves can melt Excadrill and Scizor instantly.
*   **The Play:** You must win the weather lead. If you anticipate a Charizard Y lead, keep Tyranitar in the back. Switch it in the moment the Sun is set to overwrite it with Sand. This reduces Charizard's damage and strips it of its Solar Beam's one-turn execution.

## Utilizing the Trick Room Mode

While the team is built for speed in the sand, the inclusion of Reuniclus provides a vital "Mode B." If you face a Tailwind team (usually led by Whimsicott or Murkrow), trying to outspeed them is a losing battle. 

By using a Mental Herb on Reuniclus, you ensure that it can ignore Taunt and successfully set Trick Room. Once the dimensions are twisted, your slower attackers like Tyranitar and Incineroar become the fastest threats on the field. This flexibility is what separates top-tier players from the rest of the ladder.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Pokemon Champions Unseen Fist affect moves like Earthquake?**

A: No. The "Unseen Fist" mechanic, represented by the Piercing Drill ability on Mega Excadrill, only applies to contact moves. Earthquake is a spread, non-contact move, so it will still be blocked by Protect or Wide Guard. Use Drill Run or Iron Head for contact-based attacks.

**Q: What is the best way to deal with Mega Charizard Y?**

A: The best strategy is to utilize Tyranitar’s Sand Stream as a reactive switch-in. By switching Tyranitar in after Charizard has already entered the field, you overwrite the Sun with Sand, effectively neutralizing Charizard’s Fire-type damage boost and its ability to use Solar Beam in a single turn.

**Q: Why is Scizor used alongside Mega Excadrill?**

A: Scizor provides essential priority with Bullet Punch and serves as a secondary Mega option if the matchup doesn't favor Excadrill. Additionally, Scizor’s access to Bug Bite allows it to consume the opponent's berries, which is a powerful utility in the item-restricted Regulation M environment.

**Q: Is Choice Scarf mandatory on Garchomp?**

A: In the current 2026 meta for Pokemon Champions, Choice Scarf is one of the few ways to ensure you outspeed naturally fast threats like Dragapult or boosted attackers. While not strictly mandatory, it provides a much-needed safety net for the team's speed control.
