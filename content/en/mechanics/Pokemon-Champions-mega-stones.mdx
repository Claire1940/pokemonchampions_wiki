## Step-by-Step: Equipping Your Mega Stones

The process for attaching **Pokemon Champions mega stones** to your team is notoriously hidden behind several menu layers. Unlike previous games where you might equip items directly from a bag, Pokemon Champions requires you to manage held items through the Box and Party system.

Follow these specific steps to equip your items:

1.  **Access the Box:** Open your main menu and navigate to your Pokemon Box.
2.  **Move to Party:** You can only equip items to Pokemon that are currently in your active party. Move your desired Pokemon (e.g., Charizard or Garchomp) into a party slot.
3.  **Open Held Items:** Hover over the Pokemon in your party and press the **X button**.
4.  **Navigate the Tabs:** This is where it gets tricky. The top of the screen will show categories like "Recent" and "Stat Boost."
5.  **Use the R Stick:** Use the Right Stick (R) to scroll horizontally through the tabs. You must head all the way to the right until you find the specific **Megastone** tab.
6.  **Confirm Attachment:** Select the appropriate stone and confirm to attach it to your Pokemon.

## Managing VP and Training Tickets

Victory Points (VP) are the primary currency in Pokemon Champions, and while they are used for many things, you should be extremely careful not to waste them on basic training. In 2026, the most efficient way to build a competitive team is by using Training Tickets.

If you purchased the Starter Pack, you likely have 50 Training Tickets. These allow you to change moves, abilities, and natures without spending a single point of VP. Save your VP for purchasing rare items or unlocking new Pokemon in the recruitment section.

## Connecting Pokemon Home to Champions

To bring your legacy Pokemon into the 2026 arena, you must link your account to Pokemon Home. Many players find that the "Recruitment" section will claim Home isn't linked even if you have the app. To fix this, you must go into the Pokemon Home application itself and select the **sixth icon**, which represents Pokemon Champions.

Once linked, you can transfer up to 30 Pokemon at a time. This is the fastest way to get high-IV Pokemon ready for Mega Evolution, though you will still need to acquire the specific Mega Stones within the Champions game itself to activate them in battle. For more details on the transfer process, check out the [official Pokemon Home connectivity guide](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/) for the latest 2026 updates.

> **💡 Tip:** Always check the "Achievements" tab after importing a team from Home. You might already be close to completing win-streak goals with your transferred favorites!

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Pokemon Champions mega stones tab in the menu?**

A: To find the tab, go to your Party, press X for Held Items, and use the Right Stick (R) to scroll past "Recent" and "Stat Boost" until you reach the "Megastone" category at the far right.

**Q: Can I use more than one Mega Stone per battle?**

A: While you can equip Mega Stones to multiple Pokemon in your party, you are limited to activating Mega Evolution for only one Pokemon during a single match.

**Q: How do I get more VP quickly in 2026?**

A: The fastest way is to complete the Single and Double battle tutorials for 20,000 VP. After that, focus on the Achievements tab; winning 10 battles with different Pokemon provides 500 VP each.

**Q: Why isn't my Mega Stone working in battle?**

A: Ensure the stone is actually equipped via the Party menu. Also, remember that some Pokemon require specific moves to be known, or they must be in their base form before the Mega Button will appear on the move selection screen.
