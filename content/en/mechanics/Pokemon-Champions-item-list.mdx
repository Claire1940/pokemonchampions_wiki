## The Pokemon Champions Item Economy: Understanding VP

In Pokemon Champions, the primary way to interact with the **Pokemon Champions item list** is through the use of Victory Points (VP). This currency is earned exclusively through gameplay, ensuring a level playing field where microtransactions cannot buy competitive advantages. You earn VP by completing daily logins, finishing ranked matches (win or lose), and clearing weekly missions.

Daily missions typically grant 500 VP and 24 Quick Coupons, provided you log in, recruit a Pokemon, and participate in at least one ranked battle. Weekly missions offer even larger rewards for specific feats, such as landing critical hits or using super-effective moves.

| Item Category | VP Cost Range | Usage |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Berries** | 400 VP | Consumable held items for health or status recovery |
| **Standard Held Items** | 700 - 1,000 VP | Permanent boosts (e.g., Focus Sash, Mystic Water) |
| **Mega Stones** | 2,000 VP | Required for Mega Evolution in battle |
| **Battle Music** | 5,000 VP | Cosmetic audio from previous mainline games |
| **Cosmetics** | Variable | Outfits, throwing styles, and victory poses |

> **💡 Tip:** Prioritize purchasing staple held items like the Focus Sash or Sitrus Berry early on, as these are versatile across many different team archetypes.

## Complete Pokemon Champions Item List: Held Items & Berries

At launch, the selection of items in Pokemon Champions is more curated than in previous generations. This smaller pool is a deliberate choice for Regulation M-A, forcing players to find creative solutions without some of the "power creep" items from the past. Below is the current known inventory for the competitive shop.

### Essential Held Items
These items are the backbone of the current 2026 meta. While the list is expected to expand in future updates, these are the current staples:

*   **Focus Sash:** Prevents a one-hit KO if the user has full HP.
*   **Sitrus Berry:** Restores 25% of the user's max HP when health drops below 50%.
*   **Mystic Water / Charcoal / Miracle Seed:** Boosts the power of specific elemental moves by 20%.
*   **Safety Goggles:** Protects the wearer from weather damage and powder-based moves (like Spore).
*   **Rocky Helmet:** Deals damage to the attacker upon contact.

## Training and Utility Items

Beyond held items used in battle, the **Pokemon Champions item list** includes several utility items used for "Recruitment" and "Retraining." These are vital for optimizing the stats of the Pokemon you bring in from [Pokemon Home](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/) or recruit from the daily pool.

### Training Costs Table
If you don't have a Training Ticket, you must use VP to modify your Pokemon's competitive viability.

| Service | Cost | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stat Point (EV)** | 5 VP per point | 330 VP to fully train one Pokemon's stats |
| **Nature Change** | 500 VP | Modifies the stat growth (e.g., Jolly, Adamant) |
| **Ability Change** | 500 VP | Switches between standard and Hidden Abilities |
| **Move Change** | 250 VP | Teaches a new move (1,000 VP for a full set) |

### Recruitment Items
*   **Quick Coupons:** Used to refresh the daily recruitment pool. One coupon reduces the 22-hour timer by 1 hour.
*   **Teammate Tickets:** Used for permanent recruitment of a Pokemon without spending 2,500 VP.
*   **Training Tickets:** Replaces the VP cost for stat training or ability changes.

## The "Missing" Items: Regulation M-A Meta Impact

One of the most discussed aspects of the current **Pokemon Champions item list** is what *isn't* there. As of April 2026, several classic competitive items are notably absent from the shop. This has created a unique environment for Regulation M-A, which runs until June 16, 2026.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not plan builds around the Assault Vest, Choice Band, Life Orb, or Flame Orb yet. These items are currently unavailable in the shop and cannot be used in Regulation M-A.

The absence of Choice items (Band, Specs, Scarf) means that speed tiers and damage calculations are vastly different than they were in Scarlet and Violet. Without the Life Orb, "glass cannon" attackers have slightly less explosive power, leading to a meta where positioning and Mega Evolution timing are more critical than ever. It is widely expected that these items will be added during Regulation M-B, which will host the 2026 World Championships.

## How to Get Items Faster

While the **Pokemon Champions item list** is primarily accessed via VP, there are three main ways to accelerate your progress through optional purchases:

1.  **Starter Pack ($6.99):** A one-time purchase providing 50 Training Tickets and 30 Teammate Tickets. This is the most cost-effective way to jumpstart a competitive team.
2.  **Battle Pass ($9.99/season):** Provides "Season Points" (SP) which unlock tiers of rewards, including exclusive Mega Stones and cosmetics.
3.  **Membership ($4.99/mo):** Grants access to exclusive missions that award extra Teammate and Training tickets, effectively allowing you to save your VP for shop-exclusive held items.

## FAQ

**Q: Where can I find the Choice Scarf in the Pokemon Champions item list?**

A: Currently, the Choice Scarf and other Choice items are not available in the Regulation M-A item pool. They are expected to be added in future updates or during the transition to Regulation M-B.

**Q: Can I transfer items from Pokemon Home into Pokemon Champions?**

A: No. While you can transfer Pokemon from Pokemon Home, they do not bring their held items with them. You must purchase items within Pokemon Champions using VP.

**Q: Do Mega Stones consumed in battle need to be repurchased?**

A: No, Mega Stones are permanent held items. Once you purchase a stone from the **Pokemon Champions item list**, you can swap it between any eligible Pokemon in your boxes.

**Q: What is the fastest way to earn VP for items?**

A: The fastest way is to complete your Daily and Weekly missions. Winning ranked battles provides more VP than losing, but simply participating in the ladder is enough to steadily build your currency.
