## Understanding the Innards Out Mechanic

The Innards Out ability functions as a final deterrent. When a Pokémon with this ability is knocked out by a direct attack, it deals damage to the attacker equal to the amount of HP it had remaining before the fatal blow. In the 2026 competitive circuit, this is often paired with Focus Sash or high-HP investment to ensure the return damage is high enough to secure a "one-for-one" trade against legendary threats.

## Synergizing with Ursaluna and Starraptor

To make a **Pokemon Champions Innards Out** team viable, you cannot rely solely on passive damage. You need heavy hitters that can capitalize on the holes left by your revenge-killers. Ursaluna and Starraptor have emerged as the gold standard for this "Burn and Boom" playstyle.

### The Final Gambit Starraptor Strategy
Starraptor serves as a secondary "Innards Out" style attacker by utilizing **Final Gambit**. By maximizing HP and using a Choice Scarf, Starraptor can outspeed Mega Gengar and delete a key threat instantly.

1.  **Max HP EVs:** Ensure Starraptor has the highest possible HP pool.
2.  **Choice Scarf:** Outspeed the 130+ Speed tier.
3.  **Intimidate Lead:** Lower the opponent's Attack before sacrificing Starraptor.
4.  **Clean Up:** Bring in Ursaluna or Mega Chimecho once the field is thinned.

### Ursaluna in Trick Room
Once a trade has been made via Innards Out or Final Gambit, Ursaluna enters the field under Trick Room. With a Flame Orb activated, its Guts-boosted Facade or Earthquake can sweep through remaining teams. Since Mega Chimecho (in its non-Mega form) has Levitate, you can fire off Earthquakes without harming your own teammate.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when Mega Evolving Chimecho. In some versions of Pokemon Champions, Mega Chimecho loses Levitate in favor of the Orchestra ability. Ensure your timing is correct before clicking Earthquake with Ursaluna.

## Countering the Meta

While the **Pokemon Champions Innards Out** strategy is potent, it is not without weaknesses. Experienced players will often use "chip damage" to reduce the effectiveness of the retaliation.

*   **Status Conditions:** Burn and Poison damage do not trigger Innards Out. If a Pokémon faints from status damage at the end of a turn, the ability does not activate.
*   **Substitute:** Hitting a Substitute will not trigger the ability, as the Pokémon itself did not take direct damage.
*   **Protective Pads:** This item can sometimes prevent contact-based triggers, though Innards Out is generally based on the fainting mechanic rather than contact.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Pokemon Champions Innards Out work against spread moves like Earthquake?**

A: Yes. If a spread move knocks out the user, the ability will trigger. However, the damage is only dealt to the Pokémon that used the move, even in double battles.

**Q: Can I use Focus Sash to guarantee a kill with Innards Out?**

A: Absolutely. This is a common tactic. By surviving with 1 HP and then being knocked out on the following turn (or by a second hit of a multi-hit move), you can ensure you return the maximum amount of damage possible based on your total HP pool.

**Q: Is Mega Chimecho better than Bronzong for Trick Room?**

A: In the 2026 Pokemon Champions meta, Mega Chimecho is often preferred for its offensive presence. While Bronzong is bulkier, Mega Chimecho's "Orchestra" ability allows it to deal significant damage with sound moves, preventing it from being passive on the field.

**Q: How do I handle Dark-types when using a Psychic-heavy Trick Room core?**

A: This is where Ursaluna and Starraptor are vital. Using Final Gambit to remove a Dark-type threat early, or using Ursaluna's Guts-boosted Facade, allows you to bypass the type disadvantage that Psychic setters like Chimecho face.
