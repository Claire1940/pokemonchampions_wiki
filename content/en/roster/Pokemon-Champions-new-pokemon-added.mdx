## Managing Your Roster: How Pokemon Champions New Pokemon Added via Recruitment Works

The primary way to expand your team within the game itself is through the Recruitment feature. This replaces the traditional "catch 'em all" loop with a curated selection process. Every 22 hours, the game offers a pre-selected pool of 10 different Pokemon. These specimens come with variable stats, moves, and abilities, though they are not always optimized for high-level play.

When you see a **Pokemon Champions new pokemon added** to your daily recruitment pool, you have two main paths to take: Trial Recruitment and Permanent Recruitment.

| Recruitment Type | Cost | Duration | Customization |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trial Recruitment** | 0 VP (Free) | 7 Days | None (Moves/Stats Locked) |
| **Permanent Recruitment** | 2500 VP or 1 Ticket | Forever | Full (Retraining Enabled) |
| **Quick Refresh** | 100 VP or 1 Coupon | Instant | N/A |

Trial recruitments are excellent for testing a specific niche or type-coverage without committing your hard-earned VP. However, if you find a specific Pokemon that fits your core strategy, upgrading it to a permanent recruit is essential. Permanent recruits allow for full retraining of EVs, IVs, natures, and movesets, which we will cover in the training section below.

> **💡 Tip:** Always check the recruitment pool for Shiny Pokemon or those with rare Marks. These feature a special animation in the lineup, making them easy to spot before you spend your VP.

## Pokemon Home Integration: The Ultimate Expansion Tool

While the daily recruitment pool is a steady source of team members, the most efficient way to see **Pokemon Champions new pokemon added** to your collection is by transferring them from [Pokemon Home](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/). This method is highly recommended for players who have built up a collection in *Pokemon Scarlet*, *Violet*, or *Legends: Arceus*.

Transferring a Pokemon from Home to Champions does not cost any VP. Furthermore, the Pokemon retains its moves, abilities, and training from the original game. This is particularly advantageous because move-changing in *Pokemon Champions* is one of the most expensive aspects of the in-game economy.

### Key Rules for Home Transfers
*   **Visiting Status:** Pokemon transferred from Home are considered "visitors." They remain in your Home cloud but cannot be altered or moved within Home while they are active in Champions.
*   **Box Limits:** Free-to-play users are limited to 30 box spaces. This can be expanded to 80 with the Starter Pack or 1,000 with a Monthly Membership.
*   **No Return for Recruits:** It is important to note that Pokemon originally obtained via recruitment or gifts in *Pokemon Champions* cannot be moved back to Pokemon Home. Only those brought in from Home can be returned.

## Customization and Training Costs

Once you have **Pokemon Champions new pokemon added** to your permanent roster, you will likely need to tweak their stats to fit the current meta. The training system in 2026 is a reversal of previous games; while stat training is now relatively cheap and fast, changing moves and abilities requires a significant investment of VP.

| Training Action | VP Cost | Alternative Cost |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Individual Stat Point** | 5 VP | Training Ticket |
| **Full Stat Training (Max)** | 330 VP | N/A |
| **Change Nature** | 500 VP | Training Ticket |
| **Change Ability** | 500 VP | Training Ticket |
| **Change Single Move** | 250 VP | N/A |
| **Full Moveset Reset (4)** | 1,000 VP | N/A |

Because move changes cost 250 VP each, it is often more efficient to transfer a Pokemon from Home that already knows the desired moves. If you are relying solely on recruited Pokemon, prioritize those that already have at least two of the moves you intend to use in your final build.

## Competitive Battle Formats and Tiers

The heart of the experience lies in the Ranked Ladder. This is where you earn the VP necessary to fund your team's growth. The ladder is divided into several tiers, with a new "Champion Tier" added in 2026 for the most elite players.

1.  **Beginner Tier:** The tutorial phase where players learn the mechanics.
2.  **Pokeball Tier:** The start of serious competitive play.
3.  **Great Ball Tier:** Requires consistent win rates to progress.
4.  **Ultra Ball Tier:** High-level play with optimized teams.
5.  **Master Ball Tier:** The standard for competitive veterans.
6.  **Champion Tier:** The top echelon of the global leaderboard.

Each tier (excluding Beginner) consists of four stages (4 through 1). You must progress through all stages to reach the next tier. Ranked matches award VP regardless of the outcome, though winners receive a significantly higher payout. Daily missions also provide a steady stream of VP and Quick Coupons, such as "Landing a Critical Hit" or "Using a Super Effective Move."

> **⚠️ Warning:** Regulation M-A, which runs until June 16, 2026, features a very limited item pool. Staple items like Assault Vest and Choice Band are currently unavailable, making Mega Stones (2,000 VP) even more pivotal to your strategy.

## Shop Items and Economy

The in-game shop is the only place to acquire held items, and it operates entirely on VP. There are no microtransactions for currency, ensuring a level playing field for all competitors. However, players can purchase "Replica Teams," which function similarly to the rental teams of previous generations but with a catch: you must own the Pokemon and items first to "replicate" the build.

### Common Item Costs
*   **Berries:** 400 VP
*   **Standard Held Items:** 700 – 1,000 VP
*   **Mega Stones:** 2,000 VP
*   **Battle Music:** 5,000 VP (Includes tracks from all mainline generations)

## Subscription and Monetization Options

While the core gameplay is free, there are three packages available for players looking to streamline their experience.

*   **Starter Pack ($6.99):** A one-time purchase that grants 50 training tickets, 30 teammate tickets, and 50 extra box spaces. This is the best value for new players.
*   **Battle Pass ($9.99/Season):** Provides a reward track for earning Season Points (SP). Rewards include exclusive cosmetics, Mega Stones, and teammate tickets.
*   **Membership ($4.99/Month):** Designed for the hardcore competitive player. It expands box space to 1,000, provides 15 battle team slots, and unlocks exclusive daily missions for teammate and training tickets.

## FAQ

**Q: How often are Pokemon Champions new pokemon added to the recruitment pool?**

A: The recruitment pool refreshes every 22 hours. You can see 10 new specimens each day, or use a Quick Coupon/100 VP to refresh the pool instantly.

A: Currently, 6v6 Single Battles are not available in Ranked or Private modes. The game supports 3v3 Singles and 4v4 Doubles (Bring 6, Pick 4), which is the official competitive format.

**Q: Is there a way to get VP without battling?**

A: Mega Stones are currently part of the permanent shop inventory for 2,000 VP each, though specific stones may be featured as rewards in the premium Battle Pass or during Online Competitions.
