## Transferring from Pokemon Home

To build your roster without spending Victory Points (VP), linking [Pokemon Home](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/) is the most efficient method. When you bring a Pokemon over for the first time, its stats and current form are "snapshotted" into the game.

> **💡 Tip:** If you change a Pokemon's form (e.g., Deoxys Attack to Deoxys Defense) in Home and bring it back, the game treats it as a new entry with updated stat interpretations.

## Competitive Items and Training

The item pool at launch is streamlined. While we wait for the **Pokemon Champions all available legendaries** roster to expand, players must rely on standard competitive held items. Noticeably missing are "Choice" items (Band, Specs, Scarf), which are expected to arrive in the first major balance patch.

**Available Items at Launch:**
*   **Mega Stones:** Required for all supported Mega Evolutions.
*   **Focus Sash:** Prevents one-hit KOs from full HP.
*   **Type-Boosting Items:** Silk Scarf, Miracle Seed, etc. (20% boost).
*   **Berries:** Sitrus, Lum, and various super-effective reduction berries.

Retraining your Pokemon is simplified in 2026. You can use **Training Tickets** to reset EVs or swap moves instantly based on "Battle Data," which shows the most popular sets used by top-ranked players. For instance, Garchomp users can see that 98.5% of the community runs Earthquake and Stealth Rock, allowing for quick "Apply" functionality.

## FAQ

**Q: Are there any Pokemon Champions all available legendaries at launch?**

A: Technically, no traditional box-art legendaries are available. The only exception is AZ's Floette (Eternal Flower Floette), which is classified as a legendary in the game's code. More are expected to be added in future seasonal updates.

**Q: How do I get Mega Stones in Pokemon Champions?**

A: Mega Stones can be purchased in the Frontier Shop using Victory Points (VP) earned from matches, or obtained through the Battle Pass. Some stones are also rewarded for completing "Starter Missions" in the Great Ball rank.

**Q: Can I use Alpha Pokemon from Legends: Arceus?**

A: You can transfer Alpha Pokemon via Pokemon Home, but they do not currently display the Alpha mark or have a size difference in battle. They function as standard versions of that species within the Pokemon Champions engine.

**Q: Why is my Pokemon's move highlighted in red?**

A: This indicates the move is not supported in the current version of Pokemon Champions. You must use a Training Ticket or the "Retrain" menu to replace it with a supported move before that Pokemon can be used in ranked battles.
