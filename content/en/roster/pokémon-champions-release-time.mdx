## Two Paths to Building Your Team

The game offers two distinct methods for acquiring battle-ready Pokémon. The developers have designed this system to bridge the gap between long-time fans with massive collections and new players starting fresh in 2026.

### 1. Pokémon HOME Integration
By linking your Pokémon HOME account, you can transfer legacy Pokémon from the RPG series (such as *Pokémon Scarlet & Violet*) and *Pokémon GO*. This is the fastest way to bring your battle-hardened partners into the new arena. However, keep in mind that certain moves or abilities may be adjusted to fit the competitive balance of this specific title.

### 2. The Daily Recruit System
For those who prefer to build a team within the game’s ecosystem, the Recruit system is the primary path. Once per day, you are presented with a random selection of Pokémon. 
*   **Temporary Use:** Initially, recruited Pokémon are only available for one week.
*   **Permanent Unlock:** By spending Victory Points (VP), you can permanently add a recruited Pokémon to your collection.
*   **Multiple Recruits:** While you get one free recruit per day, you can use VP to "roll" the selection again if you are looking for a specific type or species.

## Customization and Victory Points (VP)

Victory Points are the lifeblood of your progression. Every match you participate in, win or lose, grants you a certain amount of VP. This currency is used for almost every aspect of team optimization.

### Training Your Pokémon
Gone are the days of complex EV training and IV breeding. In this new title, you use VP to directly modify a Pokémon's stats.
*   **Stat Adjustment:** Increase Attack and Speed for "glass cannon" builds or boost HP and Defense for "stalling" tactics.
*   **Move Swapping:** Instantly teach your Pokémon new moves from their available pool without needing TMs.
*   **Nature & Ability Modification:** Change a Pokémon's Nature or Ability to better suit your team's synergy.

### The Shop and Cosmetics
Beyond stats, VP is used in the shop to purchase Held Items (like Choice Band or Life Orb) and Mega Stones. You can also customize your Trainer's appearance with new clothing, unique Poké Ball throwing styles, and even unlock different battle music tracks to play during your matches.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful with your VP spending in the early game. It is tempting to buy cosmetics, but prioritizing Mega Stones and stat adjustments will yield better results in Ranked Battles.

## Advanced Battle Tactics: Team Selection

One of the most interesting mechanics introduced at the **Pokémon Champions release time** is the "Team Preview" phase. Before the first move is ever made, you and your opponent can see each other's full team of six.

1.  **Analyze the Opposition:** Look for common threats or "cores" (e.g., a Rain team using Pelipper and Archaludon).
2.  **Select Your Lead:** Choose which Pokémon will start the match to gain an immediate advantage.
3.  **Counter-Picking:** If you see a major weakness in your opponent's lineup, ensure your counter-pick is ready in the back.
4.  **The Swap Mechanic:** Don't be afraid to switch. Swapping a Pokémon in "Pokémon Champions" is a tactical move that can bait out a powerful attack from your opponent while preserving your key sweepers.

## FAQ

**Q: What was the exact Pokémon Champions release time?**

A: The game officially launched globally on April 8, 2026. It is currently available for download on the Nintendo Switch eShop and mobile app stores.

**Q: How do I get more Victory Points (VP) quickly?**

A: The most efficient way to earn VP is by participating in Ranked Battles. Even if you lose, you still receive a base amount of points, but winning streaks provide significant multipliers. Completing daily missions also offers a steady stream of VP.

**Q: Can I use Mega Evolution in every battle mode?**

A: Mega Evolution is available in most modes, including Ranked and Casual battles. However, certain "Online Competitions" may have specific rules that restrict or ban the use of Mega Stones to keep the meta fresh.

**Q: Is there a story mode in Pokémon Champions?**

A: No, this title is specifically designed as a multiplayer battle-focused game. While there are tutorials and AI practice modes, it does not feature a traditional self-contained narrative or "gym challenge" like the mainline RPG series.
