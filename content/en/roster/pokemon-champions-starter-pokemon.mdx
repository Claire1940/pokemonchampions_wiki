## The New Recruitment System Explained

In Pokemon Champions, the concept of "catching" has been replaced by "Recruitment." This system provides players with a rotating pool of creatures that can be added to their permanent collection. Every 22 hours, your recruitment hub refreshes with a lineup of 10 different species. These aren't just base-level creatures; they come with variable stats, moves, and abilities, though their initial training may not always be optimized for high-level play.

To build a solid foundation of **pokemon champions starter pokemon**, you have two primary paths:

| Method | Cost | Benefits |
|---|---|---|
| **Trial Recruitment** | **Free** | Lasts 7 days; allows you to test the Pokemon in casual battles without spending resources. |
| **Permanent Recruitment** | **2500 VP** or **1 Teammate Ticket** | Keeps the Pokemon forever and unlocks the ability to retrain stats and moves. |
| **Home Transfer** | **0 VP** | Brings in Pokemon you already own from previous games; retains stats and abilities. |

> **💡 Tip:** Always use the Trial Recruitment feature first. It allows you to test a Pokemon's synergy with your team for a full week before committing your hard-earned VP to a permanent slot.

## Top 10 Pokemon Champions Starter Pokemon to Recruit

Since the recruitment pool is randomized, you won't always see the same options. However, certain species are considered "essential" for any new player looking to climb the ranked ladder in 2026. These **pokemon champions starter pokemon** offer high utility across multiple team archetypes, from Rain and Sun teams to Trick Room compositions.

### 1. Incineroar (The VGC King)
Incineroar remains the most dominant force in competitive doubles. Its ability, **Intimidate**, immediately pressures physical attackers, while moves like **Fake Out** and **Parting Shot** provide unparalleled board control. If you see Incineroar in your daily 10, it is almost always a mandatory recruit.

### 2. Whimsicott (Speed Control)
For teams that need to move first, Whimsicott is the premier choice. With the **Prankster** ability, it can set up a **Tailwind** with priority, ensuring your heavy hitters strike before the opponent can react. It also disrupts enemies with **Encore**, locking them into useless moves.

### 3. Sinistcha (The Ultimate Support)
A newer favorite in the 2026 meta, Sinistcha provides incredible sustain. Its **Hospitality** ability heals its ally upon entry, which is game-changing in a format where every HP point counts. Its signature move, **Matcha Gotcha**, deals damage while healing itself and potentially burning both opponents.

### 4. Garchomp (Physical Sweeper)
Currently ranked as one of the top choices in the Master Ball and Champion tiers, Garchomp is a reliable **pokemon champions starter pokemon** for those who prefer an aggressive playstyle. **Earthquake** and **Rock Slide** allow it to hit multiple targets, making it a staple for spread damage.

### 5. Archaladon (Rain Team Anchor)
If you are looking to build a weather-based team, Archaladon is a must-have. In the rain, its **Electro Shot** move charges and fires in a single turn, boosting its Special Attack and dealing massive damage simultaneously.

## Managing Your Victory Points (VP)

VP is the lifeblood of your progression. Unlike previous games where money was used for items and training was done through battling or vitamins, almost everything in Pokemon Champions requires VP. You earn this currency by completing daily missions, winning ranked matches, and participating in online competitions.

Because training is expensive, you must be selective about which **pokemon champions starter pokemon** you choose to invest in. Changing a single move costs **250 VP**, while changing an ability or nature costs **500 VP**.

1.  **Log in daily** to claim your 500 VP and 24 Quick Coupons.
2.  **Complete Weekly Missions** which often require using specific types or landing critical hits.
3.  **Use Pokemon Home** to transfer pre-trained Pokemon. This is the most efficient way to save VP, as transferred Pokemon retain their moves and abilities from [official Pokemon storage services](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/).

## Customizing Your Team for Regulation M-A

The current competitive season, Regulation M-A, runs until June 16, 2026. This format is unique because the available item pool is significantly restricted. Classic items like the Assault Vest and Choice Band are currently missing, making Mega Stones and Berries even more valuable.

When selecting your **pokemon champions starter pokemon**, consider how they function without traditional power-boosting items. **Mega Charizard Y** is a top-tier pick for this regulation because it sets its own weather (Sun) and gains a massive power boost through its Mega Evolution, which only requires a Mega Stone (available in the shop for 2000 VP).

> **Warning:** Be careful when purchasing clothing or cosmetics. While customization is fun, these items use the same VP pool as Mega Stones and move changes. Prioritize your battle readiness before your fashion.

## Battle Tiers and Progression

Once you have recruited your core team, it is time to enter the Ranked Ladder. The progression system is designed to match you against players of similar skill levels, ensuring that even beginners can find fair matches.

*   **Beginner Tier:** The tutorial phase where you learn the basics of the Champions interface.
*   **Pokeball to Ultra Ball:** The standard ladder where you earn increasing amounts of VP per win.
*   **Master Ball Tier:** The gateway to high-level competitive play.
*   **Champion Tier:** A new elite tier for 2026, where the top players in the world compete for leaderboard spots.

Each tier is divided into four stages (e.g., Great Ball 4 down to Great Ball 1). Climbing these ranks is the most consistent way to farm the resources needed to expand your roster of **pokemon champions starter pokemon**.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I get shiny Pokemon through the recruitment system?**

A: Yes! When viewing your daily lineup of 10 Pokemon, there is a special animation and icon if a Pokemon is shiny or has a rare mark. These can be recruited just like any other Pokemon.

**Q: What happens to my Trial Recruitment Pokemon after 7 days?**

A: After the 7-day period ends, the Pokemon will leave your roster unless you choose to upgrade it to a permanent recruit using VP or a Teammate Ticket. You cannot retrain or change the moves of a Trial recruit.

**Q: Is it better to recruit or transfer from Pokemon Home?**

A: Transferring from Home is generally better for saving VP. Since changing moves in Pokemon Champions is the most expensive part of training (250 VP per move), bringing in a Pokemon that already has the correct competitive moveset from a previous game like Scarlet or Violet is highly efficient.

**Q: How do I get more Teammate Tickets for my pokemon champions starter pokemon?**

A: Teammate Tickets are primarily earned through the Battle Pass, special event missions, or by purchasing the Starter Pack in the in-game shop. They allow you to bypass the 2500 VP cost for permanent recruitment.
