## The Return of Primal Reversion: Groudon vs. Kyogre

The "Weather Wars" have returned to the forefront of competitive play. Thanks to the Omni Ring, Groudon and Kyogre can once again assume their Primal forms, fundamentally altering the field with Desolate Land and Primordial Sea. While both are top-tier threats, Primal Groudon currently holds a slight edge in the 2026 restricted format due to its defensive typing and updated movepool.

Primal Groudon gains the Fire typing, which, when combined with Desolate Land, completely nullifies its 4x weakness to Water. Furthermore, the addition of **Heat Crash** has turned Groudon into a terrifying physical sweeper. As the third heaviest Pokémon in existence, Groudon’s STAB Heat Crash deals 120 base power damage to almost any target lighter than Ho-Oh.

| Feature | Primal Groudon | Primal Kyogre |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Typing** | Ground / Fire | Water |
| **Signature Ability** | **Desolate Land** (Nullifies Water) | **Primordial Sea** (Nullifies Fire) |
| **Key New Move** | Heat Crash / High Horsepower | Chilling Water / Liquidation |
| **Best Ally** | Flutter Mane / Chien-Pao | Urshifu-Rapid / Tornadus |
| **Meta Role** | Physical Tank / Sun Setter | Special Sweeper / Rain Setter |

> **💡 Tip:** Pair Primal Groudon with Chien-Pao. The **Sword of Ruin** ability lowers the opponent's defense, allowing Groudon's Precipice Blades to one-shot even the bulkiest resists.

Primal Kyogre remains a special attacking powerhouse, but the prevalence of Wide Guard in 2026 makes its spread moves—Water Spout and Origin Pulse—easier to predict. To counter this, many top-tier players are opting for physical Liquidation sets or relying on Tornadus with **Bleakwind Storm**, which gains 100% accuracy in the rain.

## Mega Rayquaza and the Omni Ring Advantage

Mega Rayquaza is often cited as the most powerful entity among all **pokemon champions legendary pokemon**. Unlike other Mega Evolutions, Rayquaza does not require a Mega Stone, allowing it to hold items such as **Clear Amulet** (to ignore Intimidate) or **Loaded Dice**. 

In 2026, the introduction of **Scale Shot** and **U-Turn** has given Rayquaza the flexibility it previously lacked. Scale Shot, when paired with Loaded Dice, provides a multi-hit STAB move that also boosts Rayquaza's speed, making it nearly impossible to outpace after a single turn.

### Recommended Mega Rayquaza Sets

1.  **The Physical Sweeper:** Dragon Ascent, Extreme Speed, Scale Shot, and Protect. (Held Item: Loaded Dice)
2.  **The Mixed Attacker:** Dragon Ascent, Draco Meteor, Earth Power, and Extreme Speed. (Held Item: Life Orb)
3.  **The Pivot:** Dragon Ascent, U-Turn, Extreme Speed, and Swords Dance. (Held Item: Clear Amulet)

## Psychic Superstars: Mega Mewtwo and Ultra Necrozma

While Calyrex-Shadow has dominated the Psychic-type niche for years, the Omni Ring allows Mega Mewtwo and Ultra Necrozma to reclaim their thrones. Mega Mewtwo Y boasts a staggering 194 Special Attack, making it the premier user of **Expanding Force** in Psychic Terrain. However, it faces stiff competition because it cannot hold an item while Mega Evolved, whereas a Life Orb-equipped Calyrex may still output higher raw damage.

Ultra Necrozma offers a more versatile alternative. Its ability, **Neuroforce**, increases the damage of super-effective hits, and its signature Z-Move, **Light That Burns the Sky**, ignores the opponent's abilities. In the 2026 "restricted" meta, Ultra Necrozma functions as a powerful anti-meta pick that can bypass the defensive abilities of creatures like Multiscale Dragonite or Unaware Dondozo.

## The Aura Trio: Xerneas, Yveltal, and Mega Zygarde

The Gen 6 trio remains incredibly relevant due to their "Aura" abilities which boost the damage of every Pokémon on the field. Xerneas is arguably the most dangerous, as it does not rely on a gimmick to reach its peak power. By using a **Power Herb** with **Geomancy**, Xerneas can double its Special Attack, Special Defense, and Speed in a single turn.

In 2026, the ability to **Terastallize** Xerneas has pushed it over the edge. A Tera-Fairy Xerneas under Fairy Aura creates a damage multiplier that can one-shot almost any non-Steel type. Conversely, Tera-Ground provides the perfect coverage to handle the Steel and Poison types that usually check it.

### Mega Zygarde: The 2026 Game Breaker

The most discussed addition to the **pokemon champions legendary pokemon** roster is the potential emergence of Mega Zygarde (often referred to as Zygarde-Complete). If implemented with its *Legends ZA* movepool, Zygarde gains access to **Thousand Waves**, a move that defies the standard rules of engagement.

> **Thousand Waves Stats:** 200 Base Power, 100% Accuracy, ignores Protect, ignores Wide Guard, and ignores Fairy-type Dragon immunity.

If this move remains unnerfed, Mega Zygarde will likely become the single most used Pokémon in the format. Analysts suggest that the only way to balance this is by implementing a "recharge" turn similar to Hyper Beam or significantly reducing the base power in doubles formats.

## Essential Support for Legendary Teams

No legendary is an island. To succeed in Pokémon Champions, your restricted Pokémon need a supporting cast that can handle utility, speed control, and redirection.

*   **Incineroar:** The undisputed "GOAT" of support. With Intimidate, Fake Out, and Parting Shot, it creates the safe switches your legendaries need to set up.
*   **Grimmsnarl:** The premier screen setter. Prankster Reflect and Light Screen allow glass cannons like Mega Mewtwo Y to survive long enough to sweep.
*   **Sinistcha:** A vital partner for bulky legendaries like Zygarde or Groudon. Its **Hospitality** ability heals its partner by 25% upon switching in, while Rage Powder provides necessary redirection.
*   **Farigiraf:** Essential for blocking priority moves. Its **Armor Tail** ability prevents opponents from using Fake Out or Extreme Speed against your setup sweepers.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the Omni Ring for pokemon champions legendary pokemon?**

A: The Omni Ring is typically obtained through the endgame questline involving the Paradox Research Lab. Once equipped, it allows a single Pokémon on your team to use a generational gimmick (Mega, Z-Move, or Primal) alongside Terastallization.

**Q: Can I use two legendary Pokémon on the same team?**

A: Most "Restricted" formats in 2026 allow for exactly two legendary Pokémon per team. However, certain "Special" events may limit this to one or expand it to three. Always check the current season rules in the Battle Stadium.

**Q: Is Mega Rayquaza better than Calyrex-Shadow in 2026?**

A: It depends on your team's needs. Mega Rayquaza offers better physical priority and weather control, while Calyrex-Shadow provides faster special spread damage. Rayquaza is generally more self-sufficient thanks to its ability to hold an item.

**Q: What is the best counter to Mega Zygarde?**

A: Currently, the best counters are bulky Steel-types like **Archaludon** with Stamina or **Kingambit** with Defiant. Using Wide Guard (if Zygarde is nerfed) or Misty Terrain to reduce Dragon-type damage is also a viable defensive strategy.
