## Official Mega Evolution Rules and Mechanics

The core loop of Mega Evolution remains familiar to veteran players but features specific constraints in this latest title. To Mega Evolve, a Pokemon must hold its corresponding Mega Stone, and the trainer must select the Mega Evolution icon before choosing a move. 

> **💡 Tip:** Once a Pokemon Mega Evolves, the "Mega Slot" for your team is consumed. Even if your Mega Pokemon faints, you cannot Mega Evolve another team member for the remainder of the battle.

This restriction places a premium on the survival of your Mega-capable Pokemon. Because you are locked into a single transformation, your team's item slots must be managed carefully. Since Mega Stones cannot be removed or swapped during battle, the "opportunity cost" of bringing a Mega Pokemon is significant.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I use Z-Moves or Dynamax alongside Mega Evolution in Pokemon Champions?**

**Q: Is there any way to Mega Evolve two Pokemon if one faints?**

**Q: Why do some Mega Evolutions have the same ability as their base form?**

A: Some Pokemon, like Mega Drampa (Berserk) and Mega Crabominable (Iron Fist), retain their original abilities but receive significant stat boosts. While this has been a point of contention for some players, the increased base stats are intended to compensate for the lack of a new ability.
