## Understanding the Recruitment System

Recruitment in the 2026 edition of the game is split into two primary paths: importing existing collections via Pokemon Home or using in-game resources to meet new lineups. Each method has distinct advantages, but the core goal remains the same—securing flexible "glue" pieces that fit into multiple team archetypes.

### Recruitment via Pokemon Home
If you have a pre-existing collection in Pokemon Home, this is the most efficient way to jumpstart your career. 

1.  **Link Your Account:** Ensure your support ID in the game matches your Home profile.
2.  **The "Visit" Mechanic:** Pokemon sent to this game are technically "visiting." While they are active in your roster, they are locked from being accessed in other titles like Scarlet or Violet.
3.  **No VP Cost:** Importing from Home is free of Victory Point costs, making it the preferred method for obtaining standard meta threats.

### In-Game Recruitment Shop
For those starting fresh or looking for specific 2026 variants, the recruitment shop offers rotating lineups. You can use **Victory Points (VP)** or **Swift Tickets** to secure permanent additions to your boxes.

| Recruitment Type | Cost | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trial Recruitment** | Low VP | Temporary access to a Pokemon; cannot be modified or customized. |
| **Permanent Recruitment** | High VP | Full ownership; allows for EV training, move set changes, and item equipping. |
| **Ticket Recruitment** | 1 Swift Ticket | Guaranteed permanent recruitment without spending currency. |

> **💡 Tip:** Avoid Trial Recruitment. It is a "trap" for new players. Saving for Permanent Recruitment is always more cost-effective in the long run as it allows for full competitive optimization.

## The Rotom Trap: A Warning for 2026 Players

One of the most frustrating aspects of the 2026 recruitment pool is the **Rotom** mechanic. Unlike previous games where you could swap forms at will in a catalog, forms in this title are **permanent and random**.

If you recruit a Rotom-Wash, it is stuck as a Rotom-Wash forever. If you roll a Rotom-Fan (widely considered the weakest form), you cannot change it to Heat or Mow. Because of this, if you see a high-tier form like **Rotom-Wash** or **Rotom-Heat** in your recruitment lineup, you must prioritize it. The odds of rolling the specific form you need later are statistically low.

## Farming Swift Tickets and VP

To afford the best Pokemon, you need a steady stream of currency. The game provides several avenues for this:

1.  **Daily Missions:** Simple tasks like "Win 1 Battle" or "Recruit 1 Pokemon" provide a small amount of VP.
2.  **Starter Missions:** These are one-time rewards for new players. Completing the tutorial and your first five ranked matches will grant enough Swift Tickets to secure a full team of six.
3.  **Weekly Challenges:** These are more difficult (e.g., "Win with a Sun Team 5 times") but offer the highest VP payouts.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not spend all your VP on recruitment. You will need significant funds in the Shop to purchase essential competitive items like **Citrus Berries**, **Mental Herbs**, and **Focus Sashes**.

## FAQ

**Q: Which Pokemon should I choose as my free starter in the tutorial?**

A: Palafin is widely considered the best choice. It is a top-tier offensive threat that fits into almost any team, whereas other starters like Arcanine or Gengar are easier to find in standard recruitment pools.

**Q: Can I change the moves of a recruited Pokemon?**

A: Yes, but only if you chose **Permanent Recruitment**. Trial Pokemon are locked to the moves they had when you found them. Permanent recruits can use TMs or the Move Reminder in the hub area.

**Q: How often does the recruitment lineup reset?**

A: The lineup resets every 24 hours automatically. However, you can use a **Refresh Ticket** or spend a small amount of VP to force a new lineup if you are looking for a specific meta threat.

**Q: Is it better to use Pokemon Home or recruit in-game?**

A: For the **Pokemon Champions recruit first** strategy, Home is superior for building your initial base. Use in-game recruitment specifically for 2026 exclusive forms, Megas, or when you need a specific Rotom form that you don't already own.
