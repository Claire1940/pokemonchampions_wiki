## Pokemon Champions: What Is the New Recruitment System?

In a departure from mainline titles, you do not wander through tall grass with Poke Balls. Instead, the game utilizes a "Recruitment" system. Every 22 hours, players are presented with a rotating pool of 10 Pokemon. These Pokemon come with pre-set stats, moves, and abilities, though they are not always optimized for high-level play.

There are two primary ways to secure these Pokemon for your roster: Trial Recruitment and Permanent Recruitment. Trialing a Pokemon allows you to test its viability in casual matches for a week without spending any resources. However, to customize the Pokemon’s stats or use it in high-stakes ranked play indefinitely, you must commit to a permanent recruitment.

| Recruitment Type | Cost | Duration | Customization |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trial Recruitment** | Free (0 VP) | 7 Days | None (Moves/Stats locked) |
| **Permanent Recruitment** | 2,500 VP or 1 Ticket | **Permanent** | Full (Nature, Moves, Stats) |
| **Home Transfer** | Free (0 VP) | **Permanent** | Full (Retains Home training) |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check the daily recruitment pool for Shiny Pokemon or those with rare Marks. These are highlighted with a special animation and can be permanently recruited just like standard variants.

## Integrating with Pokemon Home

For long-time fans, the most efficient way to build a roster is through Pokemon Home. You can transfer your existing collection directly into Pokemon Champions at no VP cost. A unique feature of this system is that the Pokemon technically remains in your Home cloud storage while "visiting" Champions. While they are active in the battle sim, they cannot be moved within the Home app, but any training you perform in Champions does not overwrite their original stats in the Home database.

Managing your box space is critical. Free-to-play users start with a limited capacity, while various packs and memberships expand this significantly for power users.

| Account Status | Box Space Limit | Battle Team Slots |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Free-to-Play** | 30 Pokemon | 3 Teams |
| **Starter Pack** | 80 Pokemon | 3 Teams |
| **Membership** | **1,000 Pokemon** | **18 Teams** |

## The Victory Point (VP) Economy and Training

Victory Points (VP) are the lifeblood of Pokemon Champions. Unlike other modern titles, VP cannot be purchased with real-world currency; it must be earned through gameplay. You gain VP by completing daily and weekly missions, participating in ranked battles, and climbing the ladder. 

Training in this game is the inverse of previous generations. While maxing out a Pokemon’s Effort Values (EVs) used to be the most tedious part, it is now the cheapest. Conversely, changing a Pokemon's move set is the most expensive investment.

### Training Cost Breakdown

| Training Action | Cost in VP | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stat Point (EV)** | 5 VP per point | 330 VP to fully max a Pokemon |
| **Nature Change** | 500 VP | Use to optimize Speed or Power |
| **Ability Change** | 500 VP | Includes Hidden Abilities |
| **Move Change** | 250 VP per move | **1,000 VP** for a full 4-move reset |
| **Mega Stone** | 2,000 VP | Essential for specific Mega Evolutions |

## Battle Modes and Ranking Tiers

Pokemon Champions offers four distinct ways to play. Ranked Battles are the standard for competitive growth, awarding VP based on your performance and current tier. Casual Battles provide a stress-free environment to test "Replica Teams"—the 2026 version of rental teams that require you to own the Pokemon but allow you to mirror a professional's stats and items with a single click.

The ranking system is more robust than in previous years, introducing the prestigious **Champion Tier** for the elite 1% of players globally.

1.  **Beginner Tier:** The tutorial phase where you learn the mechanics.
2.  **Pokeball Tier:** Stages 4 through 1.
3.  **Great Ball Tier:** Stages 4 through 1.
4.  **Ultra Ball Tier:** Stages 4 through 1.
5.  **Master Ball Tier:** The gateway to professional play.
6.  **Champion Tier:** The final tier for the highest-rated players on the ladder.

> **⚠️ Warning:** While 3v3 Singles and 4v4 Doubles are fully supported, the traditional 6v6 Singles format is currently unavailable in private and ranked matches. Plan your team compositions accordingly.

## Regulation M-A and the 2026 Meta

The current competitive season operates under **Regulation M-A**, which is scheduled to run until June 16, 2026. This regulation is unique because of its restricted item pool. At launch, many staple items like the Assault Vest, Choice Band, and Life Orb are missing from the shop. This has forced a meta-shift where Berries (400 VP) and Mega Stones (2,000 VP) take center stage.

The North American International Championships and the upcoming World Championships will be the first major tests for this format. Players are encouraged to visit the official [Pokemon Competitive Site](https://www.pokemon.com/us/play-pokemon/) for the most up-to-date list of banned Pokemon and restricted moves under Regulation M-A.

## Monetization and Optional Content

While the core game is free and VP cannot be bought, there are three ways to spend money to enhance the experience:
*   **Starter Pack ($6.99):** A one-time purchase offering 50 training tickets, 30 teammate tickets, and extra box space.
*   **Battle Pass ($9.99):** A seasonal pass that rewards cosmetics, Mega Stones, and currency (SP) for completing matches.
*   **Membership ($4.99/mo):** Provides the maximum box space (1,000 slots) and exclusive missions for teammate tickets.

## FAQ

**Q: Pokemon Champions what is the best way to earn VP fast?**

A: The most consistent way to earn VP is by completing your daily and weekly missions. Simply logging in, recruiting a trial Pokemon, and playing one ranked match (win or lose) grants 500 VP and 24 quick coupons every day.

**Q: Can I transfer Pokemon from Pokemon Champions back to Pokemon Home?**

A: You can only transfer Pokemon back to Home if they originated from Home. Any Pokemon obtained directly through the Recruitment feature or as in-game gifts are locked to Pokemon Champions and cannot be moved to other games.

**Q: What are Replica Teams and how do they differ from Rental Teams?**

A: Unlike the old Rental system where you could use a team for free via a code, Replica Teams in Pokemon Champions require you to own the six Pokemon and the necessary items. The Replica system then automatically spends your VP or tickets to match the moves, stats, and natures of the shared team instantly.

**Q: Is there a mobile version of the game?**

A: A mobile version of Pokemon Champions has been announced for release later in 2026. It will feature full cross-play and cross-progression with the Nintendo Switch and Switch 2 versions.
