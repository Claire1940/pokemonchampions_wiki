## Understanding the Recruitment System

In a departure from mainline entries, you do not catch wild creatures in the tall grass. Instead, the game utilizes a "Recruitment" system. Every 22 hours, your recruitment pool refreshes with 10 different Pokemon. These candidates come with pre-allocated stats and abilities, though they are rarely optimized for top-tier competitive play.

There are two primary ways to bring a Pokemon into your active roster through this menu:

| Recruitment Type | Cost | Duration | Customization |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trial Recruitment** | Free (0 VP) | 7 Days | None (Moves/Stats locked) |
| **Permanent Recruitment** | 2500 VP or 1 Teammate Ticket | Forever | Full (Can retrain everything) |
| **Quick Refresh** | 100 VP per hour remaining | Instant | N/A |

Trial recruitments are an excellent way to test a specific species in casual battles before committing your currency. If you decide you like the Pokemon, you can upgrade it to a permanent recruit at any time during the seven-day window.

> **💡 Tip:** Keep an eye out for special animations during recruitment. Shiny Pokemon and those with rare Marks will have distinct visual effects in the lineup, making them easy to spot before you spend your VP.

## Pokemon Home Integration

For many players, the most efficient way to build a team is by transferring existing collections from Pokemon Home. This method is highly recommended because it bypasses the 2500 VP recruitment cost.

When you bring a Pokemon from Home into the arena, it is considered a "visiting" Pokemon. It remains in your Home cloud storage but becomes usable within the game. While visiting, the Pokemon cannot be altered within the Home app, but you can change its stats and moves within the game without affecting the original data stored in the cloud.

### Box Space Limitations
Your storage capacity depends heavily on your account status:
*   **Free Players:** 30 Box Spaces
*   **Starter Pack Owners:** 80 Box Spaces
*   **Members:** 1,000 Box Spaces

Because space is limited for free-to-play users, you must be intentional about which Pokemon you import. Prioritize "staple" competitive threats like Incineroar, Amoonguss, or Flutter Mane that fit into multiple team archetypes.

## Training and Customization Costs

Training in 2026 has seen a significant shift. In previous generations, adjusting base stats (EVs) was the most tedious task, while changing moves was relatively simple. In this game, the developers have flipped the script: stat training is affordable, but move customization is the primary "gold sink."

| Customization Action | VP Cost | Alternative Currency |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stat Point (1 EV)** | 5 VP | N/A |
| **Full Stat Training (510 EVs)** | 330 VP | 1 Training Ticket |
| **Change Nature** | 500 VP | 1 Training Ticket |
| **Change Ability** | 500 VP | 1 Training Ticket |
| **Change Single Move** | 250 VP | N/A |
| **Change All 4 Moves** | 1,000 VP | N/A |

Because move changes are so expensive (1,000 VP for a full set), it is often better to transfer a Pokemon from [Pokemon Home](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/) that already knows the competitive moves you need. This can save you thousands of VP in the long run.

## Battle Modes and Ranking Tiers

The heart of the experience is the Ranked Ladder. Unlike previous games that ended at Master Ball tier, a new "Champion" tier has been added for the elite players in 2026.

### The Ranking Ladder
Progression is divided into tiers, each containing four stages (starting at Stage 4 and climbing to Stage 1):
1.  **Beginner Tier** (Tutorial phase)
2.  **Poke Ball Tier**
3.  **Great Ball Tier**
4.  **Ultra Ball Tier**
5.  **Master Ball Tier**
6.  **Champion Tier**

Ranked matches are the primary source of VP. The amount earned per match scales based on your current tier and whether you won or lost. Even if you lose, you still progress toward daily and weekly missions, which provide the bulk of your currency.

### Casual and Private Battles
If you want to test a "gimmick" team without risking your ladder position, Casual Battles offer the same rule sets without the ranking pressure. Private Battles allow you to challenge friends directly, though currently, the 6v6 single battle format is unavailable in this mode; all matches follow the 3v3 Singles or 4v4 Doubles (VGC) formats.

## Testing with Pokemon Showdown

Before spending VP on permanent recruits or expensive move changes, many top players use the community-run Pokemon Showdown custom server. This allows you to build teams for free and test them against other players.

### Showdown Team Building Nuances
When building for the 2026 meta on Showdown, there are a few technical differences to keep in mind:
*   **Level 50 Scaling:** All battles are automatically set to level 50.
*   **IV Sets:** Ensure all IVs are set to 31 (unless running a specific 0 Speed/Attack set).
*   **The "Minus Two" Rule:** Due to the way the game calculates stats, your EV total on Showdown will often show a "-2" remainder when fully optimized. This is normal for the current engine.
*   **Mega Evolution:** Unlike recent mainline games, Mega Evolution is a core mechanic here. On Showdown, you must select the base form and ensure the correct Mega Stone is held to validate the team.

## Economy and Monetization

The game is designed to be playable without spending money, as VP (the main currency) cannot be purchased with real-world cash. However, there are several "convenience" packages available for players who want to speed up their progress.

| Package | Price | Key Benefits |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Starter Pack** | $6.99 | 50 Training Tickets, 30 Teammate Tickets, +50 Box Spaces |
| **Battle Pass** | $9.99/Season | Exclusive Mega Stones, Cosmetics, Extra VP/Tickets |
| **Membership** | $4.99/Month | 1,000 Box Spaces, 18 Battle Team Slots, Exclusive Missions |

> **⚠️ Warning:** While the Membership offers the most box space, it is a recurring cost. If you have a massive collection in Pokemon Home, the membership is almost mandatory, but casual players can easily get by with just the one-time Starter Pack purchase.

## Regulation M-A: The Launch Meta

The inaugural competitive season follows **Regulation M-A**, which runs through June 16, 2026. This format is unique because it features a restricted item pool. Many classic items like the Assault Vest, Choice Band, and Life Orb are currently unavailable in the in-game shop.

This has led to a meta where Berries and Mega Stones are the dominant held items. Success in Regulation M-A requires players to focus on "honest" gameplay—positioning, switching, and type advantages—rather than relying on the massive damage boosts provided by Choice items or Life Orbs.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I transfer Pokemon from Pokemon Champions back to Pokemon Home?**

A: Only Pokemon that were originally transferred *into* Champions from Home can be moved back. Any Pokemon obtained via the Recruitment feature or as in-game gifts are permanently locked to the game and cannot be moved to Home.

**Q: What is the fastest way to earn VP for a Pokemon Champions tutorial guide learner?**

A: The most efficient method is to complete your Daily and Weekly missions. Simply logging in, recruiting one trial Pokemon, and playing one ranked match (win or lose) grants 500 VP daily. Weekly missions can provide several thousand additional VP for specific tasks like using certain move types.

**Q: Are there microtransactions for items like Mega Stones?**

A: No. All held items, including Mega Stones, must be purchased using VP earned through gameplay. While the Battle Pass may offer some items as rewards, the primary way to obtain them is the in-game shop using non-purchasable currency.

**Q: How do Replica Teams work?**

A: Unlike the Rental Teams of the past, Replica Teams require you to actually own the Pokemon and items. When you use a Replica code, the game automatically uses your VP and resources to retrain your existing Pokemon to match the stats and moves of the shared team. It is a convenience tool for quick setup, not a way to use Pokemon you don't own.
