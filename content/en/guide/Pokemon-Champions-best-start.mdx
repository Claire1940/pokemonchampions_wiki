## Choosing Your First Permanent Recruit

Immediately after completing the tutorial, you are presented with a pivotal choice: recruiting your first permanent Pokemon. This selection is not a temporary loan; this Pokemon will stay in your roster indefinitely. For those seeking a **Pokemon Champions best start**, the choice boils down to weather control. Weather remains one of the most oppressive mechanics in the game, and starting with a high-tier weather setter provides an immediate backbone for your first competitive team.

| Pokemon | Role | Key Ability | Best Nature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Charizard** | Special Sweeper / Sun Setter | Drought (Mega Y) | Modest |
| **Tyranitar** | Physical Tank / Sand Setter | Sand Stream | Adamant |
| **Garchomp** | Physical Sweeper | Sand Veil / Rough Skin | Jolly |

While Charizard is a fan favorite, it requires some initial investment. The default recruit is often configured as a physical attacker. To optimize it for Mega Charizard Y, you must use your starting VP (Victory Points) to move stat points from Physical Attack into Special Attack and swap its nature to Modest. Alternatively, Tyranitar offers immediate utility for "Sand Goodstuff" teams, pairing perfectly with Excadrill for a high-pressure offensive core.

> **💡 Tip:** Do not rush your first recruitment. If you plan on playing a Rain or Hail team later, look for Pelipper or Abomasnow, but Charizard Y and Tyranitar currently offer the highest early-game value.

## The New Stat Formula: Goodbye EVs

The most significant shift in 2026 is the removal of the traditional EV system. In Pokemon Champions, Individual Values (IVs) are fixed at 31 for every Pokemon, removing the need for tedious breeding or "Bottle Cap" farming. Instead, players use **Stat Points (SP)** to customize their builds. This change is the foundation of a **Pokemon Champions best start**, as it allows you to pivot your team's strategy without training a new specimen from scratch.

*   **Fixed IVs:** Every stat is naturally at its maximum potential.
*   **Stat Points (SP):** A pool of points you can distribute to any stat to increase its raw value.
*   **Nature Multipliers:** Natures still provide a 10% boost to one stat and a 10% reduction to another.
*   **Implications:** Because you cannot have "Zero Speed" IVs, Trick Room strategies have been slightly nerfed, as you can no longer be "slower" than a base-speed tie through IV manipulation.

## Efficient Resource Management (VP and Tickets)

Victory Points (VP) are the lifeblood of your progression. You earn them through battles, daily missions, and weekly challenges. For a **Pokemon Champions best start**, you must spend your VP on utility before cosmetics. The Frontier Shop contains all necessary held items and Mega Stones, but they are not cheap.

| Service / Item | Cost (VP) | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Change Ability** | 500 | High |
| **Change Nature** | 500 | High |
| **Change Move** | 250 | Medium |
| **Mega Stone** | 2,000 | Very High |
| **Training Ticket** | Variable | Low (Use gifts first) |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid spending VP on music tracks or character "drip" in the first week. Each Mega Stone costs 2,000 VP, and having a diverse selection of Megas is more important for ranked play than a new outfit.

To maximize your VP income, prioritize the **Battle Tutorials**. Completing the Double Battle tutorial alone rewards a massive 10,000 VP, which is enough to purchase five Mega Stones. Furthermore, the Mega Evolution tutorial grants stones for Garchomp, Beedrill, Gyarados, and several others for free.

## Pokemon Home: The "Leasing" Strategy

For players with an existing collection, [Pokemon Home](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/) is an invaluable tool for a **Pokemon Champions best start**. You can transfer up to 30 Pokemon at a time into the game. However, it is important to note that their stats will be "reset" to fit the Champions formula. 

When you bring a Pokemon over, its EVs are wiped, and it is assigned the standard SP pool. The benefit is that you retain the Pokemon's original Nature and any rare moves it might have learned in other regions. Note that "illegal" moves—moves the Pokemon cannot learn in the current Champions Pokedex—will be flagged with a red mark and must be replaced before the Pokemon can be used in battle.

*   **Focus on Doubles:** While singles are available, the competitive rewards and ranked focus are heavily weighted toward 4v4 Double Battles.

By following these steps—optimizing your first recruit, clearing tutorials for VP, and leveraging Pokemon Home—you will bypass the "beginner trap" of wasting resources on non-essential items. The road to the Master Ball tier begins with these fundamental choices.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I reset my stat points (SP) if I make a mistake?**

A: Yes, you can reallocate SP at any time in the training menu, but it costs a small amount of VP (roughly 5 VP per point shifted). It is much cheaper than changing an entire nature or ability.

**Q: Is the Battle Pass necessary for a Pokemon Champions best start?**

A: The free tier of the Battle Pass provides the Mega Dragonite stone and several Quick Coupons, which are essential. The paid tier ($9.99) offers mostly cosmetics and the Johto starters, which are great but not strictly necessary for competitive success.

**Q: What happens to my Pokemon when I send them back to Pokemon Home?**

A: They return to their original state. Any changes made to their stats or moves within Champions do not overwrite their data in the Home cloud, allowing you to "lease" them without ruining their builds for other games like Scarlet or Violet.

**Q: How do I get more Quick Tickets?**

A: Quick Tickets are primarily earned through daily missions and as rewards in the Battle Pass. They are used to bypass the 24-hour wait time for new recruitment lineups, which is vital for building a diverse roster quickly.
