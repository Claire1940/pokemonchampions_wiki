## Strategic Stat Allocation and Retraining

One of the most praised features of Pokemon Champions is the ease of stat manipulation. If your imported Pokémon doesn't fit the current **Pokemon Champions attack stat ranking** requirements, you can use the "Retraining" menu to fix it.

1.  **Access the Retraining Menu**: Use the minus (-) button to quickly hop to the training stadium.
2.  **Use Training Tickets**: These are often rewarded through the Battle Pass or weekly missions.
3.  **Analyze Battle Data**: Use the in-game "Battle Data" feature to see what moves and stat spreads top-ranked players are using for specific Pokémon like Garchomp or Dragonite.
4.  **Apply Presets**: You can hit "Apply" on popular community sets to instantly swap moves and optimize your attack output.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Changes made to a Pokémon's stats within Pokemon Champions do not reflect back to your Pokemon Home save. The two databases remain separate after the initial import.

## Items to Boost Your Physical Attackers

While the current version of the game is missing some traditional competitive items (like Choice Band), there are several alternatives available in the Frontier Shop to maximize your position in the **Pokemon Champions attack stat ranking**.

*   **Type-Boosting Items**: Items like Silk Scarf (Normal) or Miracle Seed (Grass) provide a flat 20% power increase to specific move types.
*   **Scope Lens**: Increases the critical hit ratio, which is vital for attackers that rely on high-crit moves like "Stone Edge."
*   **Focus Sash**: Essential for "Glass Cannon" attackers like Hydreigon or Greninja, ensuring they survive at least one hit to retaliate.
*   **Mega Stones**: Mandatory for unlocking the explosive power of the top-ranked Mega forms.

## Competitive Rankings: The "Last Respects" Factor

Basculegion has emerged as a top-tier physical threat due to the move "Last Respects." While its base attack is solid, the move's power increases for every fainted ally on your team. In a 3v3 singles match, this move can reach a base power of 150. When combined with the Adaptability ability, Basculegion becomes a monster that can one-shot even the bulkiest defenders, regardless of its standing in the raw **Pokemon Champions attack stat ranking**.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get the highest attack Machamp in Pokemon Champions?**

A: You can claim a Max Attack Machamp by going to the Mystery Gift section in your mailbox and entering the code **CHAMPION**. This Machamp comes in a Cherish Ball with a competitive move set including Dynamic Punch and Bullet Punch.

**Q: Does Mega Evolution happen in order of speed?**

A: In previous games, the fastest Pokémon would Mega Evolve first. In the 2026 version of Pokemon Champions, the order appears to be randomized or based on different priority brackets, so you cannot rely on Mega Evolution order to determine speed ties.

**Q: Can I change the stats of my Pokémon for free?**

A: Yes, you can earn Training Tickets through daily and weekly missions. These tickets allow for a full renovation of a Pokémon's stats at no cost. Additionally, Victory Points (VP) earned from battles can be used to purchase more tickets.

**Q: What is the best ability for a physical attacker in the current Pokemon Champions attack stat ranking?**

A: **Huge Power** is widely considered the best ability as it doubles the raw Attack stat. However, **Scrappy** is a close second for Fighting-type attackers as it prevents Ghost-types from switching in safely to wall your damage.
