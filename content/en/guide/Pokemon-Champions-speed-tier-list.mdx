## Understanding Speed Mechanics in Pokemon Champions

Before diving into the specific rankings, it is vital to understand how speed functions in this new competitive engine. Unlike previous iterations where training was tedious, Pokemon Champions allows you to precisely allocate points using VP (Victory Points). Raising a stat point costs 5 VP, meaning a total of 330 VP is required to fully train a Pokemon's primary stats.

In the current Regulation M-A meta, speed is influenced by three primary factors: Base Stats, Held Items (specifically the Choice Scarf), and Abilities (such as Unburden or Sand Rush). Because classic items like Choice Band and Life Orb are currently absent from the shop, the Choice Scarf has become the most influential held item for speed control.

| Modifier Type | Effect on Speed | Common Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Choice Scarf** | 1.5x Multiplier | Garchomp, Basculegion |
| **Tailwind** | 2x Multiplier (4 Turns) | Whimsicott, Talonflame |
| **Unburden** | 2x Multiplier (Permanent until switch) | Sneezler |
| **Sand Rush** | 2x Multiplier (In Weather) | Excadrill |

> **💡 Tip:** Always check if your opponent has an Intimidate lead. A White Herb on a Pokemon with Unburden, like Sneezler, can trigger a speed double immediately if the opponent tries to lower your stats.

## The Pokemon Champions Speed Tier List: Regulation M-A

The following tiers categorize Pokemon based on their base speed and their potential to reach "outspeed" benchmarks under common battle conditions. This **Pokemon Champions speed tier list** focuses on the most prominent threats found in high-level tournament play during the 2026 season.

### S-Tier: The Speed Demons (Base 130+)
These Pokemon naturally outpace almost the entire unboosted metagame. They are often used as leads to set the tempo or as "cleaners" to finish off weakened opponents.

*   **Aerodactyl (Base 150):** Currently the fastest unboosted threat in Regulation M-A. With Unnerve, it prevents opponents from consuming berries, making it a perfect counter to Sitrus Berry or Pinch Berry strategies.
*   **Sneezler (Base 140):** While already incredibly fast, its Unburden ability makes it functionally the fastest Pokemon in the game once its item (usually White Herb or Focus Sash) is consumed.
*   **Mega Gengar (Base 130):** A terrifying Shadow Tag user that traps opponents while outspeeding most of the cast.

### A-Tier: High-Speed Threats (Base 100 - 129)
This tier contains the "gold standard" for speed. Most trainers aim to "speed creep" this tier by investing just enough EVs to outpace these specific base numbers.

*   **Mega Delphox (Base 115):** A rising star in the 2026 meta, Delphox is specifically positioned to outspeed base 100 threats like Mega Charizard Y and Garchomp.
*   **Garchomp (Base 102):** A staple of the format. Often paired with a Choice Scarf to surprise opponents who expect to outspeed it with Base 110s.
*   **Mega Charizard Y (Base 100):** While powerful, its Base 100 speed is considered "awkward" in the current meta, often requiring Tailwind support from a Whimsicott to remain effective.

### B-Tier: Mid-Tier & Choice Scarf Users (Base 80 - 99)
Many of these Pokemon rely on Choice Scarf or priority moves (like Sucker Punch) to compete with the faster tiers.

*   **Basculegion (Base 98):** Extremely dangerous when equipped with a Choice Scarf. Its Last Respects move becomes a nuke in the late game.
*   **Urshifu (Base 97):** Reliable speed that allows it to pressure defensive cores before they can react.
*   **Incineroar (Base 60):** While slow, its access to Fake Out allows it to manipulate the speed flow of the first turn.

## Speed Control Strategies: Tailwind vs. Trick Room

In Pokemon Champions, the battle for turn order isn't just about base stats; it’s about control. Two primary archetypes dominate the 2026 landscape: Tailwind and Trick Room.

### The Tailwind Meta
Tailwind doubles the speed of your entire team for four turns. This is currently the most popular way to make mid-tier speed Pokemon, like Mega Charizard Y, viable against S-Tier threats. Whimsicott is the premier Tailwind setter due to its Prankster ability, which gives the move priority.

### The Trick Room Counter
If you find yourself constantly losing to the **Pokemon Champions speed tier list** leaders, Trick Room is your best friend. Farigiraf and Sinistcha are excellent Trick Room setters in Regulation M-A. Farigiraf's Armor Tail ability is particularly potent because it blocks priority moves like Fake Out and Sucker Punch, ensuring your Trick Room goes up safely.

| Strategy | Key Pokemon | Best Paired With |
|---|---|---|
| **Tailwind** | Whimsicott, Talonflame | Mega Charizard Y, Garchomp |
| **Trick Room** | Farigiraf, Sinistcha | Kingambit, Ursaluna |
| **Weather Speed** | Mega Tyranitar, Pelipper | Excadrill, Palafin |

## Recruitment and Training for Speed

To hit the benchmarks required by the **Pokemon Champions speed tier list**, you must optimize your recruitment process. You can recruit Pokemon from a daily refreshing pool of 10 or transfer them from [Pokemon Home](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/) for free. 

When recruiting, look for Pokemon that already have their speed stats partially trained to save on VP. However, if you are looking for a "Slow" variant for a Trick Room team (0 Speed IVs), transferring from Pokemon Home is often better, as you can specifically hunt for those stats in previous games like Scarlet or Violet.

> **Warning:** Changing a move in Pokemon Champions costs 250 VP, which is significantly more expensive than raising a stat point. Prioritize recruiting Pokemon that already have the correct move pool to save your currency for Mega Stones.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the fastest Pokemon in the Pokemon Champions speed tier list for Regulation M-A?**

A: Naturally, Aerodactyl holds the top spot with a base speed of 150. However, when abilities are factored in, an Unburden-boosted Sneezler is significantly faster, effectively doubling its 140 base speed.

**Q: How do I increase my Pokemon's speed in Pokemon Champions?**

A: You can increase speed by spending VP (Victory Points) in the training menu. Each point costs 5 VP. You can also change your Pokemon's nature to a speed-boosting one (like Timid or Jolly) for 500 VP.

**Q: Is Choice Scarf the only way to boost speed with an item?**

A: In the current Regulation M-A (2026), the Choice Scarf is the primary speed-boosting held item available. Other classic items like the Salac Berry are also available in the shop for 400 VP.

**Q: Why is Mega Charizard Y considered slow in the 2026 meta?**

A: With a base speed of 100, Mega Charizard Y is outpaced by common threats like Garchomp (102), Mega Delphox (115), and Aerodactyl (150). It almost always requires Tailwind support to be effective against high-tier teams.
