## Understanding the Stalwart Pokemon Mechanic

The Stalwart ability is a signature trait that fundamentally changes how targeting works in Double Battles. Under normal circumstances, moves like "Follow Me" or "Rage Powder" force an attacker to target the redirection user rather than their intended mark. Similarly, abilities like Storm Drain or Lightning Rod pull specific move types toward the Pokémon with that ability. 

A **stalwart pokemon** completely ignores these effects. When a Pokémon with Stalwart uses a move, it will always hit the target the trainer selected, regardless of any redirection attempts. This makes these Pokémon incredible "snipers" in the competitive meta, capable of picking off weakened threats even when they are supposedly protected by a support partner.

### Redirection Effects Ignored by Stalwart

| Category | Name | Effect on Standard Pokémon |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Move** | **Follow Me** | Forces all single-target moves to hit the user. |
| **Move** | **Rage Powder** | Forces all single-target moves to hit the user (Powder-based). |
| **Move** | **Spotlight** | Causes all other Pokémon to target the affected Pokémon. |
| **Ability** | **Storm Drain** | Draws in Water-type moves and boosts Special Attack. |
| **Ability** | **Lightning Rod** | Draws in Electric-type moves and boosts Special Attack. |

> **💡 Tip:** While Stalwart ignores redirection, it does not bypass protection moves like Protect, Detect, or Spiky Shield. Your target can still defend themselves normally.

## Mastering the Stalwart Pokemon Ability in VGC

To use a **stalwart pokemon** effectively, you must recognize the common board states where redirection is likely. In the 2026 competitive season, redirection is often paired with a "Trick Room" setter or a fragile "Glass Cannon" attacker. By ignoring the redirection, you can eliminate the primary threat before it has a chance to move.

### Countering Common Redirection Leads
Many teams lead with Indeedee-F and a Psychic-type attacker like Gallade or Hatterene. Indeedee uses "Follow Me" to protect its partner while they set up. A Stalwart Archaludon can target the partner directly with a powerful Flash Cannon or Dragon Pulse, potentially scoring a Knock Out and disrupting the opponent's entire strategy.

### Bypassing Absorption Abilities
Stalwart also applies to moves that would be drawn in by abilities. If your opponent has a Gastrodon with Storm Drain, they might feel safe switching in a Pokémon weak to Water-type moves. However, if your **stalwart pokemon** (via Tera Water or a specific coverage move) targets that Pokémon, the Storm Drain will not trigger for redirection purposes, though the move's damage will still be calculated normally against the chosen target.

## Countering the Stalwart Strategy

While powerful, the **stalwart pokemon** strategy has its weaknesses. Experienced players will recognize a Stalwart user at the team preview screen and adjust their playstyle accordingly.

*   **Focus Sash:** Redirection users often carry a Focus Sash. Even if you target their partner, they can still provide utility if they survive a hit.
*   **Type Immunity:** Since Duraludon and Archaludon rely heavily on Dragon and Steel moves, bringing Fairy or Steel-type resists can dampen their effectiveness.
*   **Physical Attackers:** Most Stalwart users have lower Special Defense. Strong Special attackers like Flutter Mane or Chi-Yu can often threaten a KO before the **stalwart pokemon** can act.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Stalwart ignore the effects of Ally Switch?**

A: Yes. In 2026 competitive play, if an opponent uses Ally Switch to swap positions, a **stalwart pokemon** will still hit the original target in its new position, effectively ignoring the attempt to hide the target.

**Q: Can Stalwart be suppressed by other abilities?**

A: Yes, abilities like "Neutralizing Gas" (from Weezing) will negate Stalwart. If Neutralizing Gas is active on the field, your moves will once again be subject to redirection and absorption.

**Q: Is Stalwart better than Stamina on Archaludon?**

A: It depends on your team's needs. Stamina is better for long-term physical bulk, while Stalwart is a specialized tool for offensive teams that need to bypass redirection. If you find yourself constantly losing to "Follow Me" strategies, Stalwart is the better choice.

**Q: Does Stalwart affect spread moves like Rock Slide or Dazzling Gleam?**

A: Redirection only affects single-target moves. Since spread moves already hit both opponents, the **stalwart pokemon** ability does not change the behavior of these moves, though it still prevents the damage from being absorbed by abilities like Storm Drain.
