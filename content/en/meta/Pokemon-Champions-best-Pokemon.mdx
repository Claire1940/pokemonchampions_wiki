## Top Support Units for Pokemon Champions Best Pokemon

A team of six sweepers will rarely succeed in high-level play. You need "glue" Pokemon—units that provide speed control, redirection, and stat mitigation. Incineroar continues its reign as the "G.O.A.T." of support, but new additions like Sinistcha have shaken up the 2026 rankings.

### The Priority Kings
Whimsicott and Talonflame are the premier speed control options. Whimsicott utilizes **Prankster** to set priority Tailwind or Encore, while Talonflame uses **Gale Wings** to fire off priority Brave Birds or Tailwinds while at full health.

### Redirection and Healing
*   **Sinistcha:** Its signature ability, **Hospitality**, heals its ally for 25% HP upon switching in. Combined with Rage Powder for redirection and Matcha Gacha for spread burns, it is a nightmare to remove.
*   **Clefable:** A classic redirection specialist using Follow Me. It has gained new viability as a bulky fairy-type that can soak up Dragon-type attacks aimed at your Mega Salamence or Garchomp.
*   **Farigiraf:** Essential for stopping "Fake Out" cycling. Its **Armor Tail** ability blocks all priority moves directed at your side of the field, making it the perfect partner for Trick Room setters.

## Weather Synergy and Team Archetypes

To truly utilize the **Pokemon Champions best Pokemon**, you must understand synergy. Most top-tier teams fall into one of four archetypes:

1.  **Rain Teams:** Centered around Pelipper (Drizzle) and Archaludon. Basculegion thrives here with Swift Swim, becoming a late-game cleaner that few can outspeed.
2.  **Sand Teams:** Tyranitar remains the king of Sand Stream. It pairs perfectly with Excadrill, which doubles its speed in sand via Sand Rush. The new Mega Excadrill also offers the **Piercing Drill** ability to bypass protection.
3.  **Trick Room:** Designed to turn the speed stat on its head. Ursaluna is the undisputed king here, using its 50 base speed to move first and nuke opponents with Guts-boosted Facades.
4.  **Hyper Offense:** Uses Dragapult or Sneasler to apply immediate pressure. Sneasler’s Dire Claw is controversial but effective, offering a 50% chance to sleep, paralyze, or poison the target.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be wary of "Shadow Tag" Gengar when playing weather teams. If your weather setter is trapped and knocked out, you lose your primary field advantage for the rest of the match.

## How to Recruit and Train Your Team

Building your roster of **Pokemon Champions best Pokemon** is more accessible in 2026 thanks to the recruitment system and [Pokemon Home](https://home.pokemon.com/en-us/) integration.

*   **Recruitment Tickets:** You can earn permanent recruitment tickets through daily missions. Use these on "Trial" Pokemon in your box to unlock their full training potential.
*   **Victory Points (VP):** VP is the primary currency for buying Mega Stones and training items. Engage in casual battles or complete the Mega Evolution tutorial to farm VP quickly.
*   **Knowledge over Meta:** As noted by top players, a 50-60% win rate is excellent. Don't feel forced to play "boring" meta teams. Often, a well-trained favorite with a surprise move like Arcanine's *Morning Sun* or *Roar* can snatch victory from a standard meta-slave build.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the single best Pokemon in Pokemon Champions right now?**

A: While "best" is subjective, **Incineroar** is widely considered mandatory for its ability to cycle Intimidate, Fake Out, and Parting Shot. Among Megas, **Mega Gengar** and **Mega Charizard Y** currently hold the highest usage rates in Master Rank.

**Q: How do I beat the Dondozo and Tatsugiri combo?**

A: This "Commander" strategy gives Dondozo a +2 boost to every stat. To counter it, use Pokemon with the **Unaware** ability (like Clefable or another Dondozo) to ignore those boosts, or use **Haze** to reset the field stats entirely.

**Q: Are the new Megas like Mega Frostlass worth using?**

A: Yes. Mega Frostlass is a top-tier support/offense hybrid for Hail (Snow) teams. Its ability to set Snow Warning and immediately fire off 100% accuracy Blizzards makes it a formidable alternative to the traditional Mega Glalie or Abomasnow.

**Q: Can I play Pokemon Champions without Pokemon Home?**

A: Yes, you can recruit a full competitive team using the in-game recruitment shop and trial system. However, Home allows you to bring in specialized sets and Shinies from previous generations, which can save time on grinding Victory Points.
