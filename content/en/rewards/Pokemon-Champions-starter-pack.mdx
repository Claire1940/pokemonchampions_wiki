## What is the Pokemon Champions Starter Pack?

The **Pokemon Champions starter pack** is a one-time purchase designed to give new players a "boost" without implementing the predatory microtransactions often seen in mobile-adjacent titles. Priced at **$6.99 USD**, it focuses on utility items that save time rather than providing direct power that can't be earned through gameplay.

The most immediate benefit of the pack is the expansion of your storage. While free-to-play users are limited to a strict 30 box spaces, the starter pack increases this limit to 80. This is crucial for players who want to keep multiple variants of the same species for different team compositions.

### Starter Pack Contents Overview

| Item | Quantity | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Training Tickets** | 50 | Instantly fully train a Pokémon's stats or change its nature/ability. |
| **Teammate Tickets** | 30 | Permanently recruit a Pokémon from the daily pool for free. |
| **Box Space Upgrade** | +50 Slots | Increases total storage from 30 to 80 Pokémon. |
| **Exclusive Music** | 1 Track | Trainer Battle theme from *Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! & Eevee!* |

> **💡 Tip:** Save your Teammate Tickets for rare or "off-meta" Pokémon that appear in your daily recruitment pool which you don't already own in *Pokémon HOME*.

## Maximizing Your Teammate and Training Tickets

Once you purchase the **Pokemon Champions starter pack**, you need to be strategic about how you spend your 30 Teammate Tickets and 50 Training Tickets. In Pokemon Champions, the economy revolves around **VP (Victory Points)**. While you earn VP by winning (and losing) ranked matches, the costs for manual adjustments can add up quickly.

### Training Costs (VP vs. Tickets)

| Action | VP Cost | Ticket Equivalent |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Full Stat Training** | 330 VP | 1 Training Ticket |
| **Nature Change** | 500 VP | 1 Training Ticket |
| **Ability Change** | 500 VP | 1 Training Ticket |
| **Move Change (Single)** | 250 VP | N/A (VP Only) |
| **Permanent Recruitment** | 2,500 VP | 1 Teammate Ticket |

Because Permanent Recruitment is the most expensive action in the game (2,500 VP), your **Teammate Tickets** are technically the most valuable asset in the starter pack. Using all 30 tickets is equivalent to saving **75,000 VP**, which would take hundreds of matches to earn manually.

## Recruitment and Pokémon HOME Integration

The core loop of Pokemon Champions involves "Recruitment" rather than traditional catching. Every 22 hours, your recruitment pool refreshes with 10 Pokémon. 

If you have a robust collection in *Pokémon HOME*, you can "lease" your Pokémon into Champions for free. However, be aware that their Effort Values (EVs) are wiped upon entry because Pokemon Champions uses a new stat formula.

### The 2026 Stat Formula
*   **IVs:** Fixed at 31 for all Pokémon (no more 0 IV Speed/Attack hunting).
*   **EVs:** Removed entirely.
*   **SP (Stat Points):** You manually allocate points into specific stats.
*   **Natures:** Still provide a 10% buff/nerf to specific stats.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Pokémon transferred from *HOME* will retain their original stats when moved back to *HOME*, but while they are in Champions, they must follow the new SP system.

## FAQ

**Q: Is the Pokemon Champions starter pack a recurring subscription?**

A: No. The starter pack is a one-time purchase of $6.99. Only the "Membership" is a recurring monthly or yearly subscription.

**Q: Can I use the Training Tickets on Pokémon I transferred from Pokémon HOME?**

A: Yes. Training Tickets can be used on any Pokémon in your Champions boxes to instantly adjust their Stat Points (SP), Natures, or Abilities.

**Q: What happens to my box space if I buy the starter pack and then later subscribe to the Membership?**

A: The Membership will override your storage to 1,000 slots. If your Membership expires, your storage will revert to the 80 slots provided by the starter pack (rather than the default 30).

**Q: Does the starter pack include any Mega Stones?**

A: No. Mega Stones must be purchased from the in-game shop using VP (2,000 VP each) or earned through the seasonal Battle Pass. The starter pack provides the tickets necessary to recruit and train the Pokémon that use them.
