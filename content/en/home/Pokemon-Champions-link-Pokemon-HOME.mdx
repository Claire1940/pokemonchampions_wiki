## Initiating the "Send for a Visit" Process

When you decide to move a Pokémon, you aren't just moving data; you are initiating a "Visit." This is a critical distinction in the 2026 ecosystem. While a Pokémon is visiting the ranch, its status in your cloud storage is locked.

To send a Pokémon:
*   Navigate to your *Pokémon HOME* boxes.
*   Select the specific Pokémon you wish to transfer.
*   Press the **Y button** to "Send for a Visit."
*   Review the warning prompt, which explains that certain actions (like releasing or sending to other games) will be disabled while the visit is active.

### Transfer Constraints and Statuses

| Action | Status During Visit |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Transfer to other games** | **Disabled** |
| **Releasing from Box** | **Disabled** |
| **Applying Markings** | **Disabled** |
| **Trading in HOME** | **Disabled** |
| **Competitive Use in Champions** | **Enabled** |

## Collecting Your Pokémon in Pokémon Champions

Once the transfer is initiated in the cloud, you must manually "collect" your guest on the game side. This is handled through the recruitment interface within the main menu of the ranch.

1.  Open *Pokémon Champions* and navigate to the **Recruit** menu.
2.  Select the option **Collect from Pokémon HOME**.
3.  The game will check for any pending visitors linked to your Nintendo Account.
4.  Confirm the collection. The Pokémon will then appear in your active boxes.

> **💡 Tip:** When a Pokémon visits for the first time, its "Current Strength" is calculated based on its previous stats. However, because EVs and IVs function differently in this title, the game will assign a new strength value that fits the current meta.

## Managing Moves and Stat Changes

One of the most common questions regarding the **Pokemon Champions link Pokemon HOME** system involves move sets and competitive viability. Since the 2026 update, moves are generally preserved as long as they exist within the internal database of the new game. If a Pokémon knows a move that isn't supported, it may be temporarily replaced or disabled until it returns to the cloud.

| Feature | Behavior Upon Entering Champions |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Nature** | **Remains Identical** |
| **Moves** | **Preserved** (if valid in the database) |
| **Ability** | **Remains Identical** |
| **EVs/IVs** | **Converted** to "Strength" points |
| **Training Data** | **Saved locally** to the save file |

Training done within the ranch environment is unique. While you can increase a Pokémon's performance during its visit, these specific "Strength" gains do not overwrite the original EV/IV data in your cloud storage. However, if that same Pokémon visits again in the future, the game remembers its previous training progress, provided the Pokémon hasn't changed forms.

## How to Return Pokémon to Pokémon HOME

Returning your Pokémon is just as simple as bringing them in, and it remains a free service. This is particularly useful if you need to move a Pokémon back to a mainline title like *Pokémon Scarlet* or *Violet* for a specific ribbon or event.

1.  Go to the **Recruit** menu in *Pokémon Champions*.
2.  Select **Send a Pokémon back to Pokémon HOME**.
3.  Highlight the visiting Pokémon and press the **X button**.
4.  Confirm the return. The Pokémon will immediately disappear from your ranch and reappear in your cloud boxes.

Once you log back into the cloud application, you will see a **red dot** indicator on the game icon, signifying that a visitor has returned. The Pokémon will retain a special symbol in its summary page, proving it has participated in the Champions circuit.

## Troubleshooting Common Link Issues

If you are having trouble establishing the connection, there are a few common hurdles to check. Most issues stem from account mismatches or region locks.

*   **Account Mismatch:** Ensure the Nintendo Account logged into the console is the same one that holds the cloud subscription.
*   **Slow Load Times:** Both applications require a constant handshake with the [Official Pokémon Servers](https://www.pokemon.com/us/pokemon-video-games/pokemon-home/). If the servers are under heavy load, the "Checking Nintendo Account" screen may hang.
*   **Form Changes:** If you change a Pokémon's form (such as Mega Evolution or specific regional transformations) in another game, the ranch may not recognize its previous training data upon its return.

## FAQ

**Q: Does it cost VP to use the Pokemon Champions link Pokemon HOME feature?**

A: No, the transfer process is completely free. You do not need to spend any in-game currency or Victory Points to send or receive Pokémon from the cloud.

**Q: Will my Pokémon's stats change permanently after visiting?**

A: No. While the game uses a different system for determining "Strength," your original EVs and IVs are preserved in the cloud. Training done during the visit stays within the ranch save file and does not carry over to other games.

**Q: Can I send a Pokémon directly from Pokémon GO to Champions?**

A: Not directly. You must first send the Pokémon from *Pokémon GO* to *Pokémon HOME*, and from there, you can initiate a visit to the ranch.

**Q: What happens if I lose my save data while a Pokémon is visiting?**

A: Since the Pokémon is technically "locked" in the cloud during a visit, losing your local save data can be problematic. Always ensure your cloud saves are backed up via Nintendo Switch Online to prevent losing access to visiting Pokémon.
